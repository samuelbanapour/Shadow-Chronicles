Privacy Policy

Dark Sovereign RPG — Amazon Appstore edition · Last updated 12 June 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Game Dev Solo (“we”, “us”) handles information in connection with the Amazon Appstore version of the game Dark Sovereign RPG (the “Game”). By installing or playing the Game, you agree to this policy.

Information we collect

  • In-app purchases. The Game is free. If you choose to make an optional “Tribute to the Crown” tip, the purchase is processed by Amazon In-App Purchasing. Amazon handles the payment; we only receive a purchase receipt / entitlement confirming that a tip was made — we do not receive your payment card or billing details.
  • Feedback you choose to send. If you use the in-game feedback form, we receive your message and basic context about where you were in the Game (such as the chapter or scene).
  • On-device save data. Your game progress is stored locally on your device and is not transmitted to us.
  • Basic diagnostics. We may collect limited crash and performance data to keep the Game stable.

Advertising

This Amazon edition does not display ads, and no advertising identifier is collected on Amazon Fire devices. Any advertising components included in the app do not operate on Amazon devices.

How the information is used

  • To provide the Game and unlock the optional “patron” thank-you state after a tip;
  • To review and improve the Game (feedback, diagnostics).

Third-party services

Purchases are handled by Amazon under its own privacy practices — see the Amazon.com Privacy Notice. We do not sell your personal information.

Children

Dark Sovereign RPG contains mature, dark-fantasy themes and is intended for mature audiences. It is not directed to children, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 (or under 16 in the EEA). If you believe a child has provided us information, contact us and we will delete it.

Data retention & security

We keep personal information only as long as needed for the purposes above and use reasonable measures to protect it. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure.
